<div class="syllabus-section">
  <h2>1. Fundamentals of Effective Presentation</h2>
  <div class="unit">
    <p>An effective oral presentation is not just about what you say, but how you say it. Non-verbal communication plays a crucial role in establishing credibility and engaging the audience.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="syllabus-section">
  <h2>2. Key Non-Verbal Elements</h2>
  <div class="unit">
    <ul>
      <li><strong>Facial Expression:</strong> Your face should reflect the tone of your message. A friendly, open expression helps build rapport with the audience.</li>
      <li><strong>Gesture:</strong> Use your hands to emphasize points, but avoid repetitive or distracting movements.</li>
      <li><strong>Eye Contact:</strong> Maintaining eye contact makes you appear more confident and helps you monitor the audience's response.</li>
      <li><strong>Space Distancing (Proxemics):</strong> Be aware of the physical space between you and your audience. Moving slightly toward the audience can create a sense of connection.</li>
      <li><strong>Posture:</strong> Stand straight and balanced. Good posture conveys authority and helps with breath control and voice projection.</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>
