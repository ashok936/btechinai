<div class="syllabus-section">
  <h2>What is Intelligence?</h2>
  <div class="unit">
    <p>Intelligence is a multifaceted concept that involves the ability to learn, understand, and apply knowledge to solve problems, adapt to new situations, and achieve goals. It encompasses various cognitive processes, including perception, memory, reasoning, and planning.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="syllabus-section">
  <h2>Defining Artificial Intelligence (AI)</h2>
  <div class="unit">
    <p>Artificial Intelligence is the branch of computer science that aims to create systems capable of performing tasks that typically require human intelligence. There are several ways to define AI, often categorized into four perspectives:</p>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>Thinking Humanly:</strong> The cognitive modeling approach (making machines think like humans).</li>
      <li><strong>Acting Humanly:</strong> The Turing Test approach (making machines act like humans).</li>
      <li><strong>Thinking Rationally:</strong> The "laws of thought" approach (making machines think logically).</li>
      <li><strong>Acting Rationally:</strong> The rational agent approach (making machines act to achieve the best outcome).</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="syllabus-section">

  <div class="unit">
    <p>While AI seeks to emulate natural intelligence, there are key differences between the two:</p>

        <tr>
          <td><strong>Origin</strong></td>
          <td>Biological (Human Brain)</td>
          <td>Digital (Computers/Silicon)</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Learning</strong></td>
          <td>Experience & Education</td>
